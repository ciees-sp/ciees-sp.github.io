Worked Examples

Trace a single content topic from the SME outline through the tree. Each zone shows what lands there and why.

Zone Mood Disorders: Theoretical Frameworks Relaxation: Why Relaxation Works
Need to Know CBT mood-rating before/after activity. The QMHP uses this in session: ask the client to rate mood 1–10, do a pleasant activity, rate again. It is the one CBT detail that carries practical weight for this role. Attaches directly to a task, accessible as written.

Age-specific callout: Negative thinking patterns present differently by age. Youth interpret a bad test score as personal failure; adults interpret a missed job interview the same way. Same cognitive schema, different anchors. Flag for a toggled element in the module. 		Neuromuscular tension, stress response, and the mind-body connection. The QMHP teaches relaxation techniques in session and the BHT reinforces them between sessions. Both need a plain-language "why this works" to explain to clients: your body holds stress as muscle tension; relaxation techniques release it; practicing regularly trains your nervous system to calm down faster. Attaches directly to the teaching task. Jargon like "reciprocal inhibition" and "Generalized Stress Activation Response" needs distillation, but the operational content is sound.

Age-specific callout: Youth triggers section is paired with adult sidebars, but the parallels are uneven. PMR adaptations, imagery resistance, and facilitating-with-adults sidebars have no youth equivalents. The "Script for Youth" has no adult parallel. Flag both directions for balanced callout elements.
Nice to Know The four-theory integrated model. The learner does not apply neurobiological, cognitive-behavioral, information processing, or interpersonal theory directly. But they need enough context to understand why a treatment plan includes what it includes and when to refer up the chain.

Add to document: The interpersonal cycle example (withdrawn coworker → colleague stops initiating → isolation deepens depression) is readable at the audience level as written. Goes straight to the resource doc.

Rewrite and add as time allows: The neurobiology overview assumes graduate-level prerequisite knowledge (HPA axis, hormonal risk windows, atypical brain development). The learner needs the orienting concept ("mood disorders have a biological basis; family history matters") but not the mechanism. 		Majority of module content is included. Relaxation is a skill-based module where most content is practical application and already role-specific. The scripts, teaching examples, and step-by-step techniques all attach directly to tasks the learner performs.
Cut for Time Detailed neurobiology subsections. Vascular changes in older adults, sex differences in adolescent depression rates linked to puberty hormones, specific atypical brain development structures (PFC, amygdala, hippocampus). This content doesn't connect to something the learner does on the job, and it goes beyond the context they need to understand their role. Autonomic nervous system detail. Pupil dilation, digestion redirection, vagus nerve anatomy, sympathetic and parasympathetic system mechanics. The module already covers the key message (relaxation shifts the body from stress activation to calm) in plain language. This level of detail goes beyond what the learner needs to teach and reinforce the skill effectively.
