CIEES Website Redesign · Photography Direction

Homepage Hero: Three Concepts, Three Treatments

A working brief for CIEES staff review. It covers the opportunity the redesign creates for the site's imagery, a single photographic treatment proposed for the refresh, and nine candidate images for the homepage hero — three scene concepts in three editorial styles each.

The Opportunity

The redesign gives us a chance to extend the range of work the site depicts. CIEES has grown considerably in the years since the current photography was selected, and the portfolio now spans K-12 classrooms, adult learners in endorsement programs, division and school leadership, community-based practitioners and the individuals they serve, and systems-level implementation across the Commonwealth. The site's imagery can do more to reflect that breadth, so visitors from any of our partner agencies recognize their work in ours.

The proposal is to bring one consistent photographic treatment to a small number of load-bearing spots — the homepage hero first, then the four-branch cards, then select secondary pages — while letting each image's subject matter speak to a specific arm of the center's work. Treatment is universal. Content is arm-specific.

Across the site, most of the people depicted are children or the adults teaching them. The redesign is a chance to broaden that — to show CIEES staff, adult learners, school and division leadership, community-based practitioners, and the individuals and families who benefit from the work — so each of the four arms is visible at first glance.

The Proposal

The hero image is the first thing anyone sees and it sets the tone for every page below it. The brief for this image: adults doing meaningful professional work in real education settings. CIEES is a research and implementation center serving educators, agencies, and systems across Virginia — the hero can say that at a glance.

The mission and vision copy will sit as a two-column overlay on the image, so the composition needs breathing room on the left and right. Three scene concepts are on the table, each rendered in three editorial treatments for review.

The Three Treatments

Each style applies the same compositional rules — natural light, candid body language, shallow depth of field, specific people in real environments — but shifts the mood and temperature. These are the three tonal options for the site's photographic direction.

Warm Editorial

Golden natural light, saturated but natural skin tones, soft backgrounds, cardigans and earth tones. Reads as approachable, human, narrative. Best when the hero should feel inviting and story-driven.

Cool Institutional Editorial

Neutral-to-slightly-cool daylight, crisper focus, muted palette, tailored clothing. Reads as credible, rigorous, research-forward. Best when the hero should signal systems-level work and peer-to-agency audiences.

Documentary / Photo Essay

Available light, mixed temperature, a touch of grain, lived-in detail — coffee mugs, thermoses, a travel bag on the floor. Reads as journalistic, unposed, honest. Best when the hero should feel real rather than styled.

Concept A — Teacher & Coach Consultation

A real classroom after the students have left for the day. A Black veteran teacher leans against a student desk, mid-sentence. Across from her, an older white instructional coach listens, padfolio open on the desk. Reads as: CIEES supports the adults who shape education. Keeps the school setting so it connects naturally to the grant arms' work.

Warm Editorial

Golden window light. Rust cardigan, navy shirt. Warmer, more narrative — reads as a human moment first, a professional one second.

Cool Institutional

Cooler, crisper daylight. Blazer and button-down. Reads as the institutional register — credible, measured, peer-to-agency.

Documentary

Mixed overhead and window light, travel mug on the desk. Reads as overheard rather than staged.

Concept B — Three-Person Planning Huddle

A meeting room in a school administrative building. Three people around a table with a laptop, a printed data page, and a legal pad. A division-level administrator leans forward mid-sentence; a building principal listens; a program coordinator takes notes. Reads as: CIEES works with school systems, not just classrooms. Good for signaling research and implementation work.

Warm Editorial

Warm interior light, coffee mugs, active data page. Reads as a human working conversation — story-first.

Cool Institutional

Neutral daylight, stainless thermos, clean surfaces. Reads as deliberate and institutional — closest to the Work With Us register already on the site.

Documentary

Available light from the window behind the group, whiteboard with working notes visible. Reads as overheard rather than staged.

Concept C — Solo Educator, End of Day

A middle school classroom after the students have left for the day. A Black male teacher in his late 30s sits at his desk at the back of the room, viewed from a three-quarter angle behind and to one side, pen in hand, annotating student work. Reads as: quiet dignity, reflection, the individual practitioner. The most mood-forward of the three concepts and the cleanest composition for the mission/vision overlay.

Warm Editorial

Golden afternoon light, rust shirt, plant on the sill. Reads as a human moment of quiet end-of-day focus.

Cool Institutional

Cooler daylight, tailored navy button-down, leather portfolio. Reads as measured and credible — the research-minded practitioner.

Documentary

Mixed light, travel mug, sticky-note-dotted planner. Reads as overheard and in the middle of the work — not a portrait.

Reading the Options

The three concepts sit at different points on the school-to-systems spectrum. Concept A is classroom-proximate and consultation-focused. Concept B is research-proximate and systems-focused. Concept C is mood-forward and practitioner-focused — the cleanest composition for the mission/vision overlay. The three treatments cut across all three: warm editorial is the most approachable, cool institutional is the most credentialed, documentary is the most candid. The recommendation after staff review will be a single concept-and-treatment pairing for the hero, with the remaining treatments available for secondary placements so the site reads as a coherent visual system.
