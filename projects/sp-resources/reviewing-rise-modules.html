Reviewing Rise Modules

Asynchronous modules like those authored in Articulate Rise are meant to be experienced at the learner's own pace. With no instructor in the room, the design supports how learners think through the material and check their own understanding as they go: short scannable blocks, intentional repetition of key ideas, interactive elements that carry the content, and knowledge checks throughout.

In a review version, the Continue buttons that normally gate activities are turned off, so you can move through the module without completing every interaction. In the published version, learners must complete each activity before they can continue.

Calibrating Your Review Lens

Consider the following review questions.

  • Content. Does the module move through the topic in a way that helps learners understand?
  • Practice. Does the module accurately represent how the work is approached in the field?
  • Learner. Is the module clear and useful for the intended audience?
  • Knowledge checks. Do the questions accurately reflect correct practice? Is the answer feedback helpful?

A few components have limited revision options at this stage because they were approved in earlier rounds, required for accessibility compliance, or fixed by design constraints. These include:

  • Engagement activity length and type designed to meet seat time requirements
  • Video placement, topic coverage, and length, when applicable
  • Image style and function — most photos serve as visual rest points between content sections, though some are chosen to match a specific scene
  • Interactive content types in use — flashcards, hotspots, sortables, accordions, and similar features
