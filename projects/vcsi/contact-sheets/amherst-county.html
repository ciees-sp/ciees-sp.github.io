Amherst County is where career-connected learning meets community schools. Across both Amherst County High School and the Amherst Education Center, the division is working to reduce chronic absenteeism, improve academic performance, and lower discipline referrals. At the high school, students engaged in 354 work-based learning experiences in the first quarter alone, from internships and student-run businesses to supervised agricultural programs and clinical placements. Eleven colleges and universities made on-site visits or hosted field trips, giving students a firsthand look at where those pathways lead. Signature events like the Grand Opening and Community Tailgate drew families, staff, and community partners together across both sites.

At the Amherst Education Center, the division's alternative program, family engagement has grown through Open House events, monthly art classes, and Brag Day, an open celebration where students' progress and achievements take center stage. The work is supported by community partnerships ranging from the Amherst Lions Club to all four branches of the U.S. military.

Community Engagement Across Amherst

36 Events  |  2,000 Attendees

The Community Tailgate at Amherst County High School drew 311 attendees on a September evening, filling the pavilion and field with families eating together while a food truck operated in the background. The event brought law enforcement to the table as neighbors: four sheriff's deputies sat with families, eating alongside them rather than in any official capacity. Activity stations ran between the food areas, and the crowd spanned generations, from toddlers to grandparents. The tailgate was one of 36 community events Amherst hosted this year, drawing 2,000 total attendees across the division.

During College Application Week, students gathered in a classroom with college pennants lining the wall above them and a "MAP YOUR FUTURE" banner as the backdrop. Eleven colleges and universities made on-site visits or hosted field trips in the first quarter, including Washington and Lee, Longwood, Old Dominion, VCU, VMI, UVA, Liberty, JMU, University of Lynchburg, Randolph College, and Central Virginia Community College. A College Senior Night in September drew 67 attendees, including 35 students, and a FAFSA and Financial Aid Night in October drew 55. Each event moved students one step closer to a postsecondary plan, from exploring options to completing applications.
