Albemarle County is partnering with Communities in Schools (CIS) of NOVA to build family engagement at Greer Elementary and Journey Middle School, two schools where participation had historically been low. At Greer, the bilingual site coordinator fills the school's only family liaison role, sitting on the school attendance team and meeting families in community locations in their home language. At Journey Middle, the coordinator has rebuilt family programming at a school where few families were attending events. So far this year, eight community events across both schools have drawn 201 attendees.

Community partners bring enrichment directly into the classroom at Greer Elementary, where the site coordinator's outreach has connected the school with seven local organizations. "I can be myself and you'll listen," one 2nd grader said of her time with the coordinator.

"Communities in Schools has introduced a number of creative arts related activities and programming at a time when there are few outlets for the arts and creative expression. The students are responding very positively."

— Journey Middle School Leadership
