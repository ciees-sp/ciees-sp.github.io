CIS Southside

The CIS Southside Greensville County program provides comprehensive, student-centered support designed to remove barriers to academic success and improve overall student well-being. Since launching in August 2023, the initiative has expanded from Greensville County High School to include Wyatt Middle School and Greensville Elementary, strengthening its reach across the district.

A key component of the program is the Tier III Basic Needs Crisis Response system, funded through a Community Schools Grant, with $10,000 dedicated to food and laundry vouchers. As of November, 32 food vouchers and 19 laundry vouchers have been distributed, providing immediate relief to students and families facing economic hardship and helping reduce barriers to attendance.

The program is further strengthened by strategic community partnerships, including the Washington Park Association, local veterans organizations, Toys for Tots, and Chick-fil-A Emporia. These collaborations provide mentoring, family engagement opportunities, workforce exposure, and access to essential resources. Events such as Trunk or Treat and financial literacy workshops promote community connection, family stability, and long-term economic empowerment. Serving a student population where nearly all students qualify for free or reduced meals, CIS Southside addresses critical needs related to attendance, engagement, and social-emotional development. Through a combination of immediate intervention and preventative programming, the initiative supports students in staying in school, improving outcomes, and building a foundation for future success.
