CIS Tricities

Edit copy in Google Docs

Four Petersburg-area schools are partnering with Communities in Schools (CIS) of the Tri-Cities to build coordinated support for students and families. Every school runs a food pantry, clothing closet, and school supply program. At Vernon Johns Middle, that includes providing assistance to families experiencing homelessness. At Blandford Academy, students and families describe the program as a safe place to communicate core needs and daily obstacles. At Pleasants Lane Elementary, nine community events drew 471 attendees this year, the highest engagement in the division.

Across all four schools, a total of 21 community events have drawn 922 attendees this year. At Petersburg High, mentoring through Alpha Phi Alpha at Virginia State University connects students to a historically Black Greek organization at a nearby HBCU, one of 21 partnerships supporting the work across the division.

"Students and families feel that [the program] is a safe place for them to communicate their core needs and obstacles they face daily. They look to us to help provide direction, relief, and resolution. They trust us to have their best interest and to listen to them to help the best we are able."

— Melissa Meadows, Community Schools Coordinator
