CIS Tricities

Edit copy in Google Docs

Four Petersburg-area schools are partnering with Communities in Schools (CIS) of the Tri-Cities to serve as first responders for students and families in need. Every school runs a food pantry, clothing closet, and school supply program. At Vernon Johns Middle, that includes providing assistance to families experiencing homelessness. At Blandford Academy, students and families describe the program as a safe place to communicate core needs and daily obstacles. At Pleasants Lane Elementary, nine community events drew 471 attendees this year, the highest engagement in the division.

Across all four schools, a total of twenty-one community events have drawn 922 attendees this year. At Petersburg High, mentoring through Alpha Phi Alpha at Virginia State University connects students to a historically Black Greek organization at a nearby HBCU, one of twenty-one partnerships supporting the work across the division.

"Communities in Schools is not a nice to have, but a must have as we are one of the first responders to students and families in times of need or concern."

— Melissa Meadows, Community Schools Coordinator
