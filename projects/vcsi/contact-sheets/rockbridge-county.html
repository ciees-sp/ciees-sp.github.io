Branch: Basic Needs | Treatment: Division-Wide | Cohort 1

Rockbridge County is working to reduce chronic absenteeism by three to seven percentage points across all six of its schools. The division invested its community schools grant in two positions. A Family Engagement Coordinator has spent over a year providing resources, food, and connection to families across the county. An Attendance Clerk checks in with students and families when they are absent to understand what they need and provide a path back before missing days become a pattern. The division describes the two positions as its strongest investments.

In-school clinic access now brings physical and mental health services into the buildings where students already are. Food pantries operate in every school across the county, alongside clothing closets and school supply programs. The division hosts campfire nights, tailgates, and culture nights throughout its schools, drawing 432 attendees to events designed to build the kind of connection between families and schools.

"We have seen great outcomes from both of the positions that we have added. Our Family Engagement Coordinator and our attendance clerk have both paid dividends for our division and our community. Our Family Engagement Coordinator has been providing resources, suggestions, food, etc. for families across our division for over a year now. Our attendance clerk has done an excellent job with checking in with students and families when they are out or if they need anything to make coming to school easier. We have also been able to host events like campfire nights, tailgates, and culture nights throughout the division. Our supports of food pantries in our schools across the county has also had a very positive impact on students and families."

— Jason Kirby, Community Schools Coordinator
